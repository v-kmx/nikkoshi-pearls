Shrine Est. 1617

Nikko Tosho-gu Shrine

The lavishly decorated mausoleum of Tokugawa Ieyasu, the founder of the Tokugawa shogunate that ruled Japan for over 250 years.
Temple Est. 766

Rinno-ji Temple

The most important temple in Nikko, founded by the monk Shodo Shonin, housing three gilded Buddha statues representing the deities of Nikko's sacred mountains.
Shrine Est. 782

Futarasan Shrine

Originally founded by Shodo Shonin, this shrine is dedicated to the deities of Nikko's three most sacred mountains and is the oldest shrine in the area.
Mausoleum Est. 1653

Taiyuin Mausoleum

The mausoleum of Tokugawa Iemitsu, the third Tokugawa shogun. Intentionally less ornate than Tosho-gu out of respect for his grandfather Ieyasu.
Landmark Est. 1636

Shinkyo Sacred Bridge

The iconic vermillion bridge spanning the Daiya River, considered one of Japan's three finest bridges and the symbolic gateway to the sacred area.
Nature Historical

Kanmangafuchi Abyss

A serene gorge lined with the mysterious Narabi Jizo — rows of stone statues said to change in number with each visit, a hauntingly beautiful walking path.
